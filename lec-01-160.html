🎯 Exam focus: Focus on hypotheses, theoretical approaches, and the major crime-causation and prevention theories (rational choice, routine activity, broken windows, CPTED, strain, social disorganization).
  • Null hypothesis proposes no significant relationship; the alternative hypothesis proposes a significant effect.
  • Classical Theory emphasizes free will and proportional retribution as punishment.
  • Subjective (biological) and Objective (social) approaches explain crime from opposite starting points — inside the offender vs. outside in society.
  • Rational Choice, Routine Activity, and CPTED all focus on opportunity and decision-making rather than pathology.
  • Strain and Social Disorganization theories trace crime back to blocked goals and weak neighborhood structures.
🧠 Memory aid: Memory chain: Approach (bio vs. social) → Theory (why crime happens) → Prevention (how to stop it).

🎯 Exam focus: Focus on identifying the correct defense mechanism or emotional response from a short scenario — the classic 'Chito/Maria/John' item style.
  • Denial refuses to acknowledge an obvious problem (e.g., insisting there's no drinking issue).
  • Displacement redirects emotion toward a safer or unrelated target.
  • Projection attributes one's own unacceptable feelings or actions to someone else.
  • Sublimation channels an unacceptable impulse into a socially productive outlet.
  • Rationalization/Intellectualization uses logical-sounding reasoning to avoid dealing with an emotion directly.
🧠 Memory aid: Quick clue: if someone blames others, think Projection; if they redirect anger to a new target, think Displacement; if they refuse the problem exists, think Denial.

🎯 Exam focus: Focus on distinguishing anxiety-related disorders, personality disorders, and the nature-vs-nurture debate in human development.
  • Panic Disorder involves sudden, repeated episodes of intense fear with physical symptoms.
  • PTSD follows a traumatic event and includes recurring distress triggered by reminders of it.
  • OCD involves repetitive, anxiety-driven rituals such as excessive handwashing.
  • Antisocial Personality Disorder shows no guilt or remorse for harming others.
  • Nature = biology/genetics; Nurture = environment/upbringing.
🧠 Memory aid: Memory clue: Fear = reaction to real danger; Phobia = fear of a specific thing; Anxiety Disorder = excessive worry with no clear trigger.

🎯 Exam focus: Focus on distinguishing crime types (mala in se vs. mala prohibita, violent vs. economic vs. public order) and robber/offender classifications.
  • Mala in se is inherently wrong; mala prohibita is wrong only because a law prohibits it.
  • White collar crime is committed by persons of respectability in the course of their occupation; blue collar crime is committed by ordinary career criminals.
  • Professional robbers plan carefully and rely on crime as their livelihood; opportunist robbers are unplanned, all-purpose offenders.
  • Acute criminals act on impulse or passion; chronic criminals act with deliberate, rational thinking.
  • Victimless crime has no complaining party, while public order crime disrupts the functioning of society.
🧠 Memory aid: Memory chain: Nature of the act (mala in se/prohibita) → Type of crime (violent/economic/public order) → Type of offender (professional/opportunist/addict/alcoholic).
